Doula Support Improves Birth Outcomes in NYC

How the City Wide Doula Project is addressing Racial Disparities

By Alex Krales

October 17, 2022

600 New York City families will have the support of a Doula free of cost through the City Wide Doula initiative. Doulas hired by the city are now providing healthcare to pregnant women in New York City as part of an effort to address high rates of maternal and infant mortality that have plagued the community for years.

Ancient Song Doula Services, MamaGlow and Caribbean Women Health Association are helping to train and organize the doulas that have been hired by the city to help women of color in NYC give birth safely. Systematic racism has been identified as a public health issue. The City Wide Doula Initiative aims to address health disparities for women of color who are 9X more likely to die in childbirth. The World Health organization has recommended that Doulas attend births globally as direct doula support has improved outcomes for parent and child. Doulas can offer families help with preparing for a baby, stress relief techniques, knowledge of birth processes, as well as emotional and physical support.

This program aims to directly address racial birth disparities in Maternal mortality. Mayor Adams underscored the importance of doulas and Midwives in improving birth outcomes for women of color in New York. This marks an intentional shift where birth workers of color are encouraged to accompany their clients into hospitals and acknowledgement of systemic racism as a public health issue.




Education and Birth plans

Families supported by Doulas improve parental and childbirth outcomes. Ancient Song, Mamaglow and Carribean Women's health services are teaming up to provide Doulas for expecting families. Doulas educate clients about the stages of birth and how to prepare for babies arrival. Doulas assist logistics of preparation; picking up diapers, baby wipes and scheduling appointments for needed client referrals. The World Health Organization has stated doulas have positive impacts on health outcomes, including reduced pain and fewer interventions such as C-section.

Hands on Support for Birthing People

Doulas reduce Post-Partum Depression for birthing people. Doulas are trained in Hands on, non invasive pain management techniques. Doulas been tasked by Mayor Adams to reduce maternal mortality by offering personalized support before, after and during childbirth. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) estimate that 700-900 women per year die from pregnancy-related causes in the United States. Doula infrastructure means having a budget to provide necessities for clients. The New York City Department of Mental Health and hygiene is funding this program and announced that Doulas will be covered by Medicaid.

Doulas reduce preterm birth

Doulas reduce preterm birth; the leading cause of infant mortality Infant Health improved by Doula support with feeding and preparedness. Doulas assist with stress management, lactation or formula services and education to identify warning signs of high blood pressure or low oxygenation. Regular check up appointments that focus on Birthing Persons experience doulas are able to accompany clients as they navigate the dynamic landscape of United States health care and climate change.

Increased need for Community Based Health Care

Benefits of Community Health Care include include the reduction of invasive procedures. Less invasive procedures means shorter recovery periods and reduced risk of infection. United Nations commission for Human Rights reccomended the United States ensure access to affordable health care to reduce maternal mortality and ensure all people have access to reproductive health care services and information including safe and legal abortion. The UN High Commissioner stated, "access to safe, legal and effective abortion is firmly rooted in international human rights law". The abortion ban in the United States has been declared a violation of international human rights that disproportionately impact people of color.
